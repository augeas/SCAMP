1.2 Why? Are you some sort of maniac?

"Every few months we come across an amateur who, having discovered John Conway's wonderful game of LIFE, feels obliged to present the world with one more computer program to run LIFE simulations or with one more field report on LIFE's "bestiary" or ethology"." (Tommaso Toffoli [Toffoli2001])

Yes. I wrote SCAMP because I wanted to have it to play with. What I'd really like to play with is the latest itteration in the long line of cellular automata machines by Toffoli and Margolus, [ToffoliMargolus87] but there you go. Given the time I have on my hands, (virtually none) I needed a CA machine I could fiddle with on the train. Wolfram mentioned putting CAs on mobile devices on the website for his formidable tome "A New Kind of Science" [WolframNKS]. To what degree this is scholarship or vanity publishing is somewhat contraversial. The pros and cons are weighed at great length by Ray Kurzweil [RayKurzwei], more amusingly and mercifully briefly by Cosma Shalizi [CosmaShalizi], and informatively by Lawrence Gray [GrayNKS].
