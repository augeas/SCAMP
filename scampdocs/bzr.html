6.9 B-Z Reaction

This is another cyclic automata popularized by Dewdney in Scientific American as the "Hodge-Podge Machine" [MagMach], designed to simulate the Belousov-Zhabotinsky chemical reaction. The algorithm [DewdneyBZR] starts with cells in random states, the number of which is given by the "order" parameter. The 0th state is said to be "healthy", the maximum state is "ill", with other values classed as "infected". In each generation all ill cells become healthy. Healthy cells take on a state given by a/k1 + b/k2 where the fractional part of each division is discarded, a and b are the numbers of infected and ill cells and "k1" and "k2" are chosen parameters. Ill states take on a state given by s/(a+b+1) + g, again integer division is used, a and b have the same meanings, s is the sum of the states of the cell and its neighbours and "g" is the final parameter.
